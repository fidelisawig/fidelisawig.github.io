National Wastewater Roadmap Dashboard

Developing interactive data dashboards that visualize progress and trends for Indonesia's Sustainable Sanitation targets, enabling evidence-based decisions at the Ministry of Public Works leadership level.

PowerBI Policy WASH Government

Client

Kementerian Pekerjaan Umum

Role

Data Analysis & Visualization Specialist

Tools Used

  • Python

2025 - Present

The Challenge

Indonesia's Ministry of Public Works needed a comprehensive way to track and visualize the nation's progress toward sustainable sanitation goals. With data scattered across multiple provinces and departments, decision-makers lacked a unified view of wastewater management performance.

The Approach

I developed an interactive PowerBI dashboard system that consolidates data from various sources into a single, coherent visualization platform. The solution provides real-time insights into sanitation coverage, infrastructure development, and policy compliance across all Indonesian provinces.

"Data-driven decisions require not just data, but the right visualization that tells a compelling story to stakeholders at every level."

- Project Philosophy

Key Features

The dashboard includes multi-layered filtering capabilities, allowing users to drill down from national overview to provincial details. Automated data pipelines ensure that visualizations remain current, while executive summary views provide at-a-glance metrics for leadership briefings.

National overview dashboard showing key sanitation metrics

Provincial-level performance comparison

The Impact

The dashboard is now used in ministerial briefings and has become a key tool for tracking Indonesia's SDG progress in water and sanitation. It enables data-driven policy adjustments and resource allocation decisions.

Climate Risk Assessment & Watershed Analysis

Complex spatial analysis for watershed delineation and climate vulnerability studies to inform resilience action plans under the USAID Urban WASH program.

QGIS Climate USAID Hydrology

Project Background

As part of USAID's Urban WASH initiative, there was a critical need to understand climate vulnerabilities affecting water resources and sanitation infrastructure in Indonesian cities. This required comprehensive spatial analysis of watersheds and their relationship to urban settlements.

Methodology

Using Digital Elevation Models (DEM) and hydrological modeling techniques, I delineated watershed boundaries and identified areas most vulnerable to climate-related hazards such as flooding, drought, and water scarcity.

GEDSI Integration

Working closely with the GEDSI (Gender Equality, Disability, and Social Inclusion) team, I ensured that climate risk assessments incorporated participatory approaches. Community mapping sessions helped identify vulnerable populations and their specific needs in climate adaptation planning.

Disaster Risk Reduction

2017

Aceh Provincial Disaster Risk Mapping

Developing hazard and vulnerability spatial models that contributed to Aceh's Provincial Disaster Management Plan with the UN Migration Agency (IOM).

DRR IOM Aceh Hazard Mapping
Add project image here

Client

IOM - UN Migration Agency

Role

GIS Assistant

Location

Banda Aceh, Indonesia

Tools Used

  • ArcGIS
  • QGIS

Context

Aceh Province, still recovering from the devastating 2004 tsunami, required comprehensive disaster risk assessments to inform long-term resilience planning. Working with IOM, I contributed to developing spatial models that would guide provincial disaster management strategies.

Analysis Components

The project involved multi-hazard mapping including earthquake, tsunami, flood, and landslide risks. Each hazard layer was combined with population vulnerability data to create composite risk indices that identified priority areas for intervention.

"Understanding where risks intersect with vulnerable populations is the foundation of effective disaster preparedness."

- Project Approach

The spatial analysis directly informed Aceh's Provincial Disaster Management Plan, providing evidence-based recommendations for emergency shelter locations, evacuation routes, and early warning system placement.
